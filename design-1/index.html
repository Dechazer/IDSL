About Us

Who We Are

The Institute for Development and Sustainable Livelihoods (IDSL) is a national Non-Governmental Organization registered in 2024 pursuant to provisions of the Non Governmental Organizations Co-Ordination Act of 1990 (CAP 19).

Our headquarters are located in Nakuru city off Nakuru-Subukia Road at Bahati, second floor of the Grand Canyon Building, with a provisional satellite office in Nairobi at Cianda House.

We are a forward-thinking organization dedicated to sustainable agriculture, climate resilience, and equitable food systems in Kenya, working with rural communities to promote agroecology, green innovations, and policy advocacy.

Our motto: Engaging local communities for sustainability and resilience in green development

Our Vision

A thriving, food-secure society where communities live sustainably, adapt to climate challenges, and enjoy their fundamental rights to safe, nutritious food and a healthy environment.

Our Mission

To transform food systems through:

  • Sustainable farming (agroecology, soil & water conservation)
  • Climate adaptation & mitigation (renewable energy, carbon credits)
  • Humanitarian & social empowerment (WASH, gender inclusion, conflict resolution)
  • Policy & advocacy (food sovereignty, indigenous rights, environmental protection)

Why We Do It!

Kenya faces climate shocks, food insecurity, and social inequalities, particularly in marginalized regions. By integrating traditional knowledge with modern innovations, we help communities build resilience, protect natural resources, and secure their right to food (as enshrined in Kenya’s Constitution, Article 43).

Guided by the following principles, we are able to make it happen

  • Human dignity.
  • Equity and social inclusion.
  • Inclusivity.
  • Equality.
  • Human rights.
  • Good governance .
  • Sustainable development.

Our Programs

Empowering communities through sustainable solutions and innovative approaches to create lasting positive change.

Combine forces in evaluating and nurting talented farmers who are willing and capable to take part, to derive impactful change to the society.

Willing and ready to take impactful change to enhance a better nation through improved, sustainable farming.

Walk with our and discover how we make it happen!

i

Sustainable Agriculture & Food Security

Goal: Empower farmers with resilient, eco-friendly practices.

  • Promote agroecology (crop diversity, organic inputs, waste recycling)
  • Train farmers in soil erosion control & water conservation
  • Strengthen seed sovereignty & local food production
  • Reduce food loss & post-harvest waste
ii

Climate Action & Green Innovations

Goal: Combat climate change through adaptation & mitigation.

  • Advocate for renewable energy (solar, biogas)
  • Implement carbon sequestration (agroforestry, no-till farming)
  • Support climate-smart livestock & fisheries
  • Train communities in disaster risk reduction
iii

Humanitarian & Social Development

Goal: Improve livelihoods in crisis-affected areas.

  • Emergency food & water aid in droughts/floods
  • WASH programs (clean water access, sanitation)
  • Gender equality & anti-GBV initiatives
  • Peacebuilding & conflict resolution in pastoral regions
iv

Food Systems Transformation & Advocacy

Goal: Influence policies for a fairer, healthier food future.

  • Promote indigenous crops & traditional diets for better nutrition
  • Campaign against harmful agrochemicals & GMOs
  • Lobby for pro-farmer policies & land rights
  • Document & protect indigenous knowledge (ITK)

Our Approach

Community-led

Farmers, women, and youth drive our programs, ensuring local ownership and sustainable solutions.

Evidence-based

Monitoring & evaluation ensure measurable results and continuous improvement in our initiatives.

Collaborative

Partnering with governments, NGOs, and research institutions to maximize our collective impact.

Our Impact

Measuring success through tangible improvements in communities, environment, and livelihoods across Kenya.

Food security for vulnerable households through sustainable farming practices and emergency aid.

Greener farming with higher yields & lower emissions through eco-friendly agricultural methods.

Stronger communities through gender equity & conflict resolution initiatives.

Policy changes supporting sustainable development and farmer-friendly legislation.
